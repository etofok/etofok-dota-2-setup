Chat Wheel Setup

This is the setup.
As in absolutely required.

Let's discuss it.

Go and Get Back are required for any coordination.
We put them as polar opposites on the wheel (top - bottom).

Group Up serves as the "Get Ready" and "Let's Go" call-outs.

It's also useful when you actually want to group up, i.e. for Smokes or objectives.

Spread Out is important for any pre-teamfight action, especially when facing big ultimates such as Black Hole, Reverse Polarity, Chronosphere, etc.

Generally useful around objectives, especially high-ground.
Super-super important.

Fight Under Tower probably contributes to half of my MMR.

Fight Under Tower is the ultimate "DO NOT DO ANYTHING STUPID".

- Prepares the team for action.
- Reminds them not to dive.
- Reminds them not to chase off your own high-ground.

It's the best.

All miss is a fantastic call-out for:

"They are smoked" ,
"They are ganking",
"Watch the minimap",
"Careful"

I use it all the time when I don't see anyone of the minimap.
I also spam it as a morale booster after we team wipe.

It's fantastic.

Use All miss with Fight under tower, Get back and Group Up to bring your team into safety (results may vary).

I really find these two very valuable:
Nice
Space Created

I use Nice whenever something good (or lucky) happens.
By "good" I mean "in our favor".

Positive reinforcenment affects morale of the team in the direction you want.

I use Space Created when something bad (or unlucky) happens.
By "bad" I mean "not in our favor".

This phrase is iconic and hilarious, so it diffuses some of the negativity that builds up.

Somewhat in order of importance

1. Go and Get Back
2. Nice
3. All miss
4. Fight Under Tower
5. Space Created
6. Spread Out
7. Group Up

I haven't heat-mapped the usage of my chat wheel phrases.
What am I - a savage?

But I thought about the order of importance.
And I feel like it really depends on your role, general playstyle and also skill level.

So - it depends. But as long as you have them all - it doesn't matter.

You can alt-click the in-game timer to call-out current game time into chat.

This means you don't have to put it on your chat wheel.
