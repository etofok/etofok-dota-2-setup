Mouse

A few personal notes on the mouse.

DPI

Over the course of my life I went from 1600 DPI to 1200 to 1000 to 800 to 1600.

400-800-1600-3200 DPI "step ups" is probably the most common DPI range baked into mice.
600-1200-2400 is also quite common, but less so.

You can notice they double every step up, starting from a different base.

One thing for sure - by sticking with the most common DPI range you can easily switch between devices or use other people computers.

Weird and unusual DPI settings require extra software installation - which may be impossible in many situations, i.e. at work.

Or you have to bring your own devices everywhere, which again, may or may not be possible.
For me, it's just not practical.

The best part - you can literally practice your aim while doing office work, because your sensitivity is same between the devices you use.

But what sensitivity is best?

Long story short I've been using 1600 for the past couple of years and I'm probably going to stick with.

It is slightly too fast for my liking for daily use, but for games it's probably the best.

For Dota 2 it's a no-brainer.

Mouse Side Buttons

I do not recommend binding action keys to mouse side buttons Mouse 4Mouse 5.

The mouse is for pointing.

3 reasons why I do NOT bind game actions to side buttons:
1. Your grip is worse, because you have to account for the buttons in your grip.
2. Your aim is worse, because your grip is worse.
3. Your wrist gets more strained, because your grip is worse.

I used Mouse 4 (mouse side button 1) for my Blink Dagger for a decade.

I felt like I needed that extra button, but turns out I just had a bad keyboard layout and had to compensate by finding buttons elsewhere.

If you like your mouse buttons - more power to you.
However consider these points, especially if you have some sort of wrist pain, and especially at higher sensitivities. Having had problems with the wrist I do not recommend developing problems with the wrist.

Not exclusively Dota 2 related, but I always disable Mouse Acceleration.
Yes, you can get used to having it on.
To be fair, people get used to terrible things all the time.

But personally I don't see how losing aim consistency and adding extra strain to my wrist is good.

The reason it adds to strain is because of all the 'tuning' micro adjustment you have to do at the end of your movements.
I can't explain it better that that, please accept my apologies.

So unless you're on a touchpad or trackball you probably - probably - shouldn't use Mouse Acceleration.


And don't get me wrong - I have used it for a long time when I didn't know about its existence.

That's how I developed a massive ball-like bulge in my wrist, to the point I couldn't bend my hand 30 degrees backwards.
I took 10 years to heal.

That was a long time ago, so long - I had to do 50 push-ups and 50 volleyball overhead self-throws for my PE grade in college.

Neither of which you can properly do without bending your fucking wrist.

....

To be fair I was also playing an unhealthy amount.
