Move

You want Move in these 3 "bucket" cases:

- follow and stick to an enemy unit
- follow and stick to an allied unit, always
- move your unit(s) without them attacking anything

Notable examples:

- Move allows you to stick Juggernaut Blade Fury to the target without attacking it, which would stop the movement.
This also matters for Primal Beast Trample, Pudge Rot and other similar cases.

- Move allows you to stick to an enemy trying to juke you through the trees.
This makes pathfinding work in your favor.

- Move allows you to stick your invisible units to an enemy.
For example, for vision.

- Move allows you to properly reposition your illusions and controlled units without them attacking anything on their paths.

- Move allows you to ignore the 'deniable' condition.
If you right-click a unit that can be denied - creeps below 50% or heroes under poison effects - you will attack the unit.

I'd say it's pretty helpful.

The default key is M, which is only good if you never use it.

We could do the "reverse-Attack" - AltSpacebar - but it's obscenely clunky for unit management with control groups.

123 and AltSpacebar is probably the least ergonomic pattern anyone could ever have.

We could also do the "reverse-Stop" - AltF - but in my opinion you don't want to bind AltF to anything.

Not having this bound makes your F and AltF point to Stop, just like we have bound both Normal Cast and Quick Cast to work regardless of our Alt state.


Realistically the only reasonable option here is G for Go.

  • Move G
  • Patrol H

And as a 'collateral' we bind Patrol to H.

Please notice, there's no "Quick Patrol", so it requires a left-click.
