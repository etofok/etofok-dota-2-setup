Talents

Upgrade Talent is not something that you use often.
Literally up to 8 times in a very long game.

But you do use it.

There was a period of my life when I was a Turbo enjoyer where it's not rare to get a big level up in the middle of a fight.

That's how I found out that having a hotkey for Upgrade Talent is more comfortable than not having it.

I felt annoyed I had to click the plus icon with my mouse.
This might sounds ridiculous but it's true.

Upgrade Talent is great on F1 because you then select the left-or-right talent with 1 or 2 which are right next to it.

I like the hand motion of F1 → 12.

Because F1 is at the very top of the keyboard, this motion feels like I'm reaching for an upgrade.

  • Upgrade Talent F1
  • Left Talent 1
  • Right Talent 2
  • Attributes 3   or   none

Alternatively, if you want to free up F1 you could 'demote' it to AltF1

  • Upgrade Talent ALTF1

I used this for a while.

The upside is that you don't occupy the F1F2F3F4 "logical block" with a "one-off" bind.

However, there are downsides.

1. ALTF1 -> 1 is awkward because you have to let go of ALT to "actually select".

And if you don't - you end up calling the shop actions that are bound to ALT1 and ALTF2.

2. You can't bind the "actually selecting" to ALT1 / ALT2.
Or technically - you can, but it just ends up pinging the talents into chat.

3. Ergonomically it's just as not buttery smooth as F1 -> 1 anyway.

So F1 -> 1 it is.

Please note!

Note that Select # Talent always selects the talent in order of appearance in the select talent menu - from bottom to top.

So if you have multiple talents available - for example both 10 and 15 talents - the hotkey will select the level 10 talent first.

You may also want to unbind Upgrade Attributes to prevent accidentally selecting it.
