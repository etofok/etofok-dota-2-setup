Accessibility Discussion

There's this girl with 10000 Dota 2 hours and 1000 Venomancer games.
She's disabled on her right hand.

The only left-handed mouse on the entire market is the left-handed Naga,
which is - by design - way too bulky, especially for a woman hand.

On a mouse with 2 extra side buttons you are still very constrained:

Mouse Wheel Click
Mouse Wheel Up
Mouse Wheel Down
Mouse4
Mouse5

Dota 2 has evolved into a UI powerhouse, and most things can be accessed with just the mouse.

But not all.

It's especially difficult to use targetable actions such as Glimmer Cape, Eul's Scepter of Divinity, Decrepify, False Promise or hundreds of others.

We need the Action Wheel.

You've seen this in many games.

Sometimes called the "Radial menu".
Sometimes called the "Power menu".

It's not like I'm the first to come up with this design.

This is useful because you would be able to bind things like Item Slot 1 or Ability 4 to the wheel and use these actions from the wheel.

The wheel has 8 slots - which is a massive amount of real-estate out of nowhere.
(Minus 1 as you have to bind the wheel itself.)

I made a mock-up in AutoHotkey and it works well.
Immediately useful.

However, since it's a 3rd party crutch - it's not "connected" to the game, so it can't "lock" on targets.
It's quite clunky to use on non-stationary targets.

Hard to explain,
but there's a time gap between you opening the wheel menu and selecting the action from the menu.

If that takes you 0.5 seconds and the target moves at 350ms - by the time you selected the action the target has moved under your cursor 175 units.

So you need this to be "native" to the game.

Still, works flawlessly for no-target actions, which instantly adds a lot of buttons to your brain-to-game interface.

Unfortunately, it also is a cheat, technically.
After all - it's a 3rd party assist.

So I want a native to the game solution.

Since that Venomancer lived with me for quite some time - that's how I got interested in figuring this out - I playtested my proposed one-handed setup myself quite a lot.

And while I have used both my custom wheel and the keypad mouse (it's not amazing but I can handle it) - after 20 games I felt like I was playing at ~90% of my capacity.

There are definitely some hero pool limitations, but handling a "normal" hero was a breeze.
